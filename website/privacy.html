Privacy Policy

Last updated: July 6, 2026

Pixel Art Gallery is built to work entirely on your device. The short version: no analytics, no tracking, no accounts, and your images never leave your machine unless you send them somewhere yourself.

What the app collects

Nothing. Pixel Art Gallery has no analytics, telemetry, crash reporting, advertising identifiers, or user accounts. We do not collect, store, or share any personal data.

Your images

Images you import are stored locally on your device, in the app's own storage area, along with the pixelated variants you create. They are never uploaded anywhere by the app.

Local network use

The app can discover Flaschen Taschen LED displays on your local network using standard mDNS/Bonjour browsing, and it connects to a display only when you explicitly send a variant to it. The pixel data you choose to send goes directly from your device to that display over your local network — no third-party server is involved.

Software updates (macOS)

The Mac app uses the open-source Sparkle framework to check for updates. When it checks, it fetches this site's update feed over HTTPS — a standard web request that, like any web request, includes your IP address and the app's version information in the user agent. This is used only to serve the feed and download files; we do not log or profile it, and no other data is sent. You can adjust update checking in the app's settings.

Photos access (iOS)

On iOS, the app can save exported images to your Photos library. This happens only when you initiate it, uses the system's standard permission prompt, and is limited to adding the images you chose to save. The app never reads your Photos library beyond what the system photo picker shows you when you pick an image to import.

This website

This site is a set of static pages. It uses no cookies, no analytics, and no third-party resources. The web server keeps ordinary short-lived access logs (IP address, requested page) for operational purposes only.

Changes

If this policy ever changes, the updated version will be published on this page with a new date. Given the app's design — everything stays local — meaningful changes are unlikely.

Contact

Questions about this policy? Open an issue on the project's repository or contact the developer directly.
