## Description

Umbrella for reworking the gallery screen's chrome. Today `GalleryListView` shows the `+` and `…` actions in a top toolbar, a plain "Gallery" navigation title, and a full-bleed pixelated wallpaper that renders far too dark to enjoy. The redesign: (1) a **banner header** at the top reading **"Pixel Art Gallery"** over the fun pixelated background made vibrant (not the current washed-out dark wallpaper); (2) the main content area behind the grid switches to a **simple matte background driven by system light/dark mode**; and (3) the `+`, **Settings**, and **Sort** actions move to a **bottom button bar** (custom) with a larger, centered **`+`** as the primary add-image affordance. Targets iOS primarily (see screenshot); macOS adaptation to be decided per child.

## Long Description

Grounded in the current code (`PixelArtGalleryKit/Sources/PixelArtGalleryKit/UI/GalleryListView.swift`):
- The screen is a `NavigationStack` → `ZStack { BackgroundPixelsView().ignoresSafeArea(); content }` with `.navigationTitle("Gallery")`.
- `BackgroundPixelsView` (`UI/BackgroundPixelsView.swift`) draws 8pt palette-tinted pixel tiles at **opacity 0.5**, using `Color.darkerPixelColors` in dark mode / `lighterPixelColors` in light mode — that low-opacity darkened palette is why it reads "super dark". The vibrant source palette is `Color.pixelColors` (gold `#F7B900`, etc.) in `UI/Palette.swift`.
- Top `.toolbar`: `+` (`.primaryAction`, opens the image picker via `coordinator.showImagePicker`), a **Sort** `Menu` (`.secondaryAction`, `@AppStorage("gallerySortOrder")`), and a **Settings** gear (`.secondaryAction`, iOS only — macOS uses the `Settings` scene / ⌘,).

The two halves are separable, so this umbrella has two children:
- **[#0070](0070.md)** — Banner header + matte content background: put "Pixel Art Gallery" over a vibrant pixelated banner at the top; replace the full-bleed dark wallpaper behind the grid with a system-driven matte background.
- **[#0071](0071.md)** — Bottom button bar: move `+` / Settings / Sort out of the top toolbar into a custom bottom bar with a large centered `+`.

Acceptance criteria:
- The top of the gallery is a banner reading "Pixel Art Gallery" over a visibly vibrant (not dark/washed-out) pixelated background.
- The grid sits on a plain matte background that follows system light/dark mode.
- `+` (add image), Settings, and Sort are reachable from a bottom bar; the `+` is centered and larger/prominent.
- Verified by **running the app** (iOS at minimum) — the screen visibly matches the intent, not just green builds.
